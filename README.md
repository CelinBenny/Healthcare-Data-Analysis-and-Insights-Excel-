# Healthcare-Data-Analysis-and-Insights-Excel-
Project Overview

The healthcare industry generates large volumes of data that can provide valuable insights into patient health, treatment patterns, and healthcare costs.
This project focuses on analyzing a healthcare dataset containing medical examination records, hospitalization information, and patient profiles. The goal is to explore relationships between health indicators, identify trends in patient data, and generate insights that can support better healthcare decision-making.
The analysis was conducted using Microsoft Excel, including data cleaning, transformation, exploratory analysis, and visualization.

Objectives

- Clean and prepare healthcare data for analysis

- Explore relationships between patient health indicators

- Identify trends in medical examinations and hospitalizations

- Analyze patterns in healthcare costs and patient profiles

- Create visual summaries to communicate insights effectively

Tools Used

- Microsoft Excel

- Data Cleaning

- Data Transformation

- Slicers
  
- Pivot Tables

- Data Visualization (Charts & Graphs)

Dataset Size

- Total Records: 2,335 patients

- Features: Health metrics, lifestyle indicators, medical history, hospital data, and treatment charges.

Key Insights from the Healthcare Analysis Dashboard

- Obesity is associated with the highest healthcare costs
  
-The Average Healthcare Charges by Weight & Diabetes Status chart shows that:

-Obese individuals have the highest average medical expenses.

-Normal and underweight individuals have comparatively lower costs.

This suggests that weight-related health risks significantly increase healthcare expenditure.

- Diabetes further increases medical costs

Across all weight categories:

-Patients with diabetes consistently show higher healthcare charges.

-Even among normal-weight individuals, diabetic patients have higher treatment costs.

This highlights the financial burden of chronic diseases like diabetes.

- Smoking is linked to higher cancer history prevalence
  
-The Cancer History Distribution among Smokers vs Non-Smokers chart indicates:

-A larger share of individuals with cancer history are smokers.

-Non-smokers show a significantly smaller proportion.

This suggests a potential relationship between smoking and cancer risk in the dataset.

- Hospital tier significantly affects treatment charges

The Average Charges by Hospital Tier and State chart shows:
 
 -Tier 1 hospitals tend to have the highest average charges.

 -Tier 2 and Tier 3 hospitals show comparatively lower costs.

This suggests that advanced healthcare facilities may lead to higher treatment expenses.

- Transplant patients undergo more major surgeries

The Transplant History vs Surgeries and HbA1C analysis indicates:
 
 -Patients with transplant history have higher numbers of major surgeries.

 -HbA1C levels also appear elevated among transplant patients.

This reflects the complex medical care required for transplant cases.

- Weak correlation between age and BMI

-The Age vs BMI scatter plot shows a very weak relationship between age and BMI.

This suggests that body mass index is influenced more by lifestyle factors than age alone.

- Healthcare charges increase with age

The Age vs Charges plot shows a slight upward trend:

-Older individuals tend to have higher medical expenses.

-However, the correlation appears moderate rather than strong.

This suggests age contributes to healthcare costs but is not the only determining factor.

Interactive Dashboard Features

The dashboard includes interactive slicers that allow dynamic filtering of the analysis:

- Diabetes Status Filter

Users can filter the dashboard by:

 -Diabetes

 -Prediabetes

 -Normal

This allows comparison of healthcare charges, BMI trends, and surgical history across different diabetic conditions.

- Weight Status Filter

The dashboard can also be filtered based on:

 -Normal Weight

 -Overweight

 -Obesity

 -Underweight

This helps analyze how body weight categories influence healthcare costs, BMI patterns, and medical outcomes.

- Smoker Status Filter

The dashboard also includes a slicer to filter patients based on smoking habits:

 -Yes

 -No

This filter allows users to compare healthcare trends between smokers and non-smokers, helping analyze how smoking behavior may influence healthcare charges,
cancer history distribution,BMI and health indicators,surgical and medical treatment patterns.

Using these slicers enables deeper analysis of multiple health risk factors affecting patient outcomes and medical costs.
